Adobo: The Heart & Soul of Filipino Cuisine!

Adobo is one of the most beloved Filipino dishes, celebrated for its savory, tangy, and slightly sweet flavor. Made with meat (commonly chicken or pork) simmered in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves, it embodies the heart of Filipino home cooking.

"Adobo is the soul of Filipino cooking — simple ingredients, slow simmering, and a flavor that feels like home."
– Filipino Food Enthusiast

Ingredients:

  • Chicken or Pork – 1 kg
  • Soy Sauce – 1/2 cup
  • Vinegar – 1/2 cup
  • Garlic – 6 cloves
  • Bay Leaves – 3 leaves
  • Peppercorns – 1 tsp

Preparation:

  1. Marinate meat in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and peppercorns for 30 minutes.
  2. Simmer the mixture with bay leaves until meat is tender.
  3. Allow sauce to reduce until rich and flavorful.
  4. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  5. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Cooking Tips:

  • Use a mix of chicken and pork for a richer flavor.
  • Let the Adobo rest overnight — it tastes even better the next day.
